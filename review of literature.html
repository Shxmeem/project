A Study on Factors Influencing People's Preference for UPI, Card or Cash for Daily Transactions

Chapter 2: Review of Literature
Research Project | Sameem & Hanas

2.1 Sahoo et al. (2024)

UPI's rapid adoption in India is driven by ease of use, interoperability, and security, significantly boosting financial inclusion and reducing cash dependence. The review highlights how UPI has transformed digital payments across sectors.

2.2 Dhivya et al. (2025)

Study based on UTAUT factors shows UPI's user-friendly design and trust features play a major role in adoption, with projections indicating massive future growth in transaction volumes.

2.3 RBI Study (2025)

Reserve Bank of India analysis found that rising UPI adoption correlates with reduced cash demand, indicating UPI's strong substitution effect on physical currency.

2.4 Government Report on Digital Payments (2024)

Data from the Ministry of Finance shows UPI's exponential growth in transactions over the past decade, accounting for the majority share of digital payment volume in India.

2.5 IJARESM Conclusion (2023)

Research shows UPI's significant impact on India's shift toward a cashless economy, with rapid transaction growth and widespread integration into daily financial activities.

2.6 UPI Adoption Patterns (2024)

A review article highlights UPI's inclusion of underbanked populations, expanding access to formal financial systems and aiding economic engagement beyond major cities.

2.7 RBI Annual Report (2024)

Reserve Bank data indicates UPI accounted for nearly four out of five digital transactions in India, showing its dominance over other digital modes.

2.8 ShodhKosh Study (2024)

This paper traces UPI's emergence and growth post-2016, noting its role in improving financial transparency and literacy through digital payments.

2.9 Behavioral Impact Study (2024)

Academic research indicates that mobile payments like UPI can influence spending behavior, often increasing purchase amounts compared to cash, reflecting psychological effects of payment methods.

2.10 NPCI Performance Report (2025)

NPCI's financial results reflect rising digital payment adoption and indicate UPI's central role in driving the organization's growth.

2.11 RBI Economist Study (2024)

Analysis shows that UPI has surged in P2M (person-to-merchant) usage, suggesting increasing acceptance for everyday purchases and declining reliance on cash post-pandemic.

2.12 Regional Growth Patterns (2025)

State-level data highlights strong UPI adoption in regions like Telangana, reflecting deeper integration of digital payments across different Indian states.

2.13 UPI Global Positioning (2025)

Reports suggest India's UPI surpasses major global platforms in real-time payments, underscoring its scale and influence beyond national borders.

2.14 Mobile Payments Future (2025)

Industry projections aim to bring hundreds of millions of new users into India's UPI ecosystem, indicating continued growth and reduced cash dependency.

2.15 Fintech Integration (2025)

Emerging research explores integrating UPI with broader financial services, such as credit delivery, expanding its utility beyond basic transactions.
