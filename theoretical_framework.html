A Study on Factors Influencing People's Preference for UPI, Card or Cash for Daily Transactions

Chapter 3: Theoretical Framework
Research Project | SAMEEM & HANAS

3.1 Concept of Payment Systems

A payment system refers to the methods used for transferring money in exchange for goods and services. The major payment methods used in India today are UPI, debit/credit cards, and cash.

The evolution of payment systems has improved speed, convenience, and accessibility. The choice of payment method varies based on user preference and situation. Payment systems play an important role in everyday economic activities.

3.2 Concept of UPI (Unified Payments Interface)

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is a real-time digital payment system developed by NPCI in 2016. It allows instant fund transfer between bank accounts through mobile apps. UPI supports merchant payments, bill payments, and peer-to-peer transfers.

It works 24/7 and does not require bank details to be shared. UPI has become one of the most widely used payment systems in India.

3.3 Features of Payment Methods

Key Features:
  • Instant money transfer through UPI.
  • 24/7 availability of digital payments.
  • QR code-based payments for easy merchant transactions.
  • Card payments support online and international purchases.
  • Cash provides universal acceptance without technology.

3.4 Characteristics of Payment Systems

Main Characteristics:
  • Convenience in completing transactions quickly.
  • Speed of payment processing.
  • Security and privacy in transactions.
  • Accessibility across different population groups.
  • Reliability for daily financial needs.

3.5 Importance of Payment Systems

Significance:
  • Promotes financial inclusion.
  • Reduces dependency on physical cash.
  • Supports growth of online businesses.
  • Improves transparency in financial transactions.
  • Encourages digital economy development.

3.6 Challenges of Payment Methods

Key Challenges:
  • Fear of online fraud and cybercrime.
  • Network and internet connectivity issues.
  • Lack of digital awareness among some users.
  • Technical failures such as payment declines.
  • Resistance to change due to habit of using cash.

3.7 Limitations of Payment Methods

Major Limitations:
  • UPI requires smartphone and internet access.
  • Card payments depend on POS machine availability.
  • Cash can be lost, stolen, or damaged easily.
  • Digital payments may involve service charges in some cases.
  • Limited acceptance of digital payments in remote areas.
